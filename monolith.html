Intro

Monolith is a mobile library app of the two books that I wrote. These books contained detail about Epicross, my imaginary world.

Motivation

As a child, I used to write books. Sometimes they were novels, sometimes they were comic books. Whatever I wrote, it was always about an imaginary universe called Epicross. The more I wrote them, the more detailed they became. One day, I wanted a toy based on Epicross, just like how people create sci-fi movie prop replicas. So, I decided to create two books written in the language of Epicross: a catalog of ancient weapons and the Epicross historia. I wrote these imagining as if they are actually written by the ancient people. As time passed, I realized that a digital medium will last longer than a physical one. So, I put these two books together and made a mobile library app. This is Monolith.

About Epicross

Who was your hero? Some people might have had real-life heroes. Some people borrowed from the comic books. I, as a kid, did the exact same; my favorite hero back then was X from the Megaman series. I loved my hero so much that I started writing a fan fiction based on the same universe, except the character was different. This new hobby of mine led me to ask, "Why not create my own hero?" Thus, Raid John Cleric was born, who had his own special weapon, arch-nemesis, and history. Epicross is the setting for Raid's story, and it can be anything as long as Raid is in it. For example, sometimes I imagine Raid appearing in other settings such as the Middle-Earth. In that case, Epicross would expand to include the Middle-Earth.
