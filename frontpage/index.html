"DO DILIGENCE"

Here's a story.

I thought Do Diligence was an American idiom. I remember hearing something that sounded similar and thought it spelled 'Do Diligence.' I was raised in Korea, a goal-oriented society, but my parents made sure I valued the process as much as the result. So when I heard this 'idiom,' I thought, "It must mean to be diligent until the very end. Wow. Persistence must be a universal trait. I should stick to it." Ironically, the phrase I actually heard was "Due Diligence," which means something completely different. But, thanks to this coincidence, I grew up believing that a lot can be achieved when you just persist through.
